Managing a tracking plan is hard. You have to make sure it's kept up to date to what is actually being tracked, make sure existing events and properties are reused where relevant, make sure new events and properties match with the naming conventions you have in place, communicating changes and collect feedback from the team, share implementation instructions with developers, and so on.

If you relate to any of the challenges mentioned above you might want to consider switching your tracking plan over to Avo.

The Avo Tracking Plan is a collaborative web app that will make managing your tracking plan less of a headache, with features like <Link passHref href="/audit/index"><a>Tracking Plan audit</a></Link> to ensure naming conventions are followed, <Link passHref href="/data-design/global-namespace"><a>global event and property namespace</a></Link> to ensure consistency across your events and properties, <Link passHref href="/workspace/branches"><a>branched collaboration</a></Link> with <Link passHref href="/workspace/branches#protected-main-branch"><a>approval workflows</a></Link> and <Link passHref href="/workspace/branches#share-the-implementation-instructions"><a>auto generated implementation instructions</a></Link>, <Link passHref href="/workspace/integrations#slack"><a>Slack integration</a></Link> to keep your team in the loop, <Link passHref href="/workspace/tracking-plan/publishing"><a>auto publishing</a></Link> to keep your downstream schemas in sync, and more!

When you have your tracking plan defined in Avo you can check out our <Link passHref href="/implementation/avo-functions-overview"><a>code generated, type-safe, functions</a></Link> for developers to increase tracking implementation speed and reliability, and tracking observability with the <Link passHref href="/data-design/start-using-inspector"><a>Inspector</a></Link> so you know how your _actual_ tracking compares to the tracking plan.

In this guide we'll walk through the process of setting up your tracking plan in Avo, suggesting your first changes on a branch, inviting a team member and asking for a review. If you're looking for a deeper dive into the tracking plan design capabilities of the Avo Tracking Plan, check out our <Link passHref href="/data-design/start-data-design"><a>deep dive guide on data design in Avo</a></Link>.

### Step 1: Create an Avo workspace

Head to our <a href="https://avo.app/onboarding" target="_blank">onboarding flow</a> and setup a new workspace.

If you have an existing tracking plan that you want to import, you can do so by clicking the **Import** button in the workspace's settings.

### Step 4: Invite a colleague and ask them for a review

Now we're ready to share our suggestions with our team, and ask them for input. Go to your workspace settings and invite a team member. Workspace members can be one of admin, editor, comment only or view only. To learn more about members and roles, check out our <Link passHref href="/workspace/members"><a>members docs</a></Link>.
